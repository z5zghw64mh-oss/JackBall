Sustainable Aviation Fuels

by Jack Ball

What is SAF?

SAF is a type of jet fuel that isnt made from oil. It works in regular airplane engines which is the big deal because airlines dont have to change anything. It can cut carbon emissions by up to 80% depending on how its made.

How is it Made?

There are a few ways to make it:

  1. HEFA - cooking oil and animal fats
    • most common right now
    • works today at scale
    • not enough supply to grow much bigger
  2. Fischer-Tropsch - converts garbage and wood waste into fuel
    • way more feedstock available
    • very expensive to build the plants
  3. Alcohol to Jet - ferments crops or waste into alcohol then converts it
    • can use existing ethanol infrastructure
  4. Power to Liquid (e-fuels) - pulls CO2 from the air and uses green hydrogen
    • nearly zero emissions if done right
    • super expensive right now
    • needs tons of cheap renewable electricity

Does it Have a Future?

Aviation is about 2-3% of global CO2 emissions and its really hard to decarbonize. Batteries are too heavy for long flights and hydrogen needs totally new infrastructure. SAF is the most realistic option we have right now.

Good signs:

  • EU requires 6% SAF by 2030 and 70% by 2050
  • US has tax credits pushing production
  • Airlines are already signing purchase agreements
  • New plants being built around the world

Problems:

  • Costs 3 to 5 times more than regular jet fuel
  • Only covers about 0.5% of fuel needs today
  • Not enough feedstock to scale without e-fuels
  • E-fuels need renewable electricity that doesnt exist at that scale yet
Bottom line: SAF is not a perfect solution but its the best one aviation has. If the investment and policy keep going it could cover most of what aviation needs by 2050.
